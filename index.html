The Travels

March 17th, 2017

Sapporo, Japan. I don't think I have ever fallen in love with a city view so quickly. Upon first glance (when landing) it seems like any Japanese city. Lots of shops, many places to eat interesting food, and everyone driving on the left.

However...

Once you get to the top of the tower at the JR station, you see one of the most magnificent views imaginable. A prestine city covered in thawing snow in front of a mountain range that I could have just looked at for hours.

Aside from the wonderful view, Tower 38 (the name of the building of the JR tower) has a wonderful coffee shop on the top level so you can enjoy a nice mocha whatever as you bathe your eyes in a scenic landscape of human production. One quirky feature is the interesting bathroom on the 38th floor. Unfortunately, you have to be male, but on the top floor, you have a urinal with a spectacular view of the city once again. It's a bit odd considering that the feeling of using the restroom is a bit like peeing off the building itself. quite surreal considering that's probably what a lot of guys think of when they go to a tall building (I know I'm one of them). If city views arent your thing then I would suggest the wide array of artisian restuarants, tea houses, coffee and cake shops around the city.

During the two days that I was in Sapporo and Jozenkei (the mountain town nearby) there was a whole array of shops, restuarants, tea shops and cafes that all have their own style and flavor. From contemporary to modern, a wide selection of shops is there, and hopefully you have a diet plan on standby.
